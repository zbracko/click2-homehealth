Common Questions

Home Healthcare FAQ

How quickly can home healthcare services begin?

In most cases, a registered nurse can be in your home within 24–48 hours of physician referral. For urgent hospital discharges, we offer same-day clinical assessments and can begin skilled nursing within hours across South Florida.

What clinical credentials do your staff hold?

Our clinical team includes Registered Nurses (RN), Licensed Practical Nurses (LPN), Physical Therapists (PT), Occupational Therapists (OT), Speech-Language Pathologists (SLP), and Certified Nursing Assistants (CNA). All hold active Florida licenses and undergo Level 2 FBI/FDLE background checks, annual competency evaluations, and continuing education.

Does Medicare cover home healthcare?

Yes. Medicare covers home healthcare if your physician certifies you are homebound and require skilled nursing or therapy services. We are Medicare-certified and handle all documentation, authorizations, and billing. Many patients pay $0 out-of-pocket for qualifying services.

What about Medicaid and private insurance?

We accept most Medicaid managed care plans (Humana, Molina, Sunshine Health, etc.), most major private insurance carriers, long-term care insurance, and private pay. Our intake team verifies benefits and explains coverage before services begin — no surprises.

What's the difference between home health and home care?

Home healthcare is clinical and medically-focused — skilled nursing, IV therapy, wound care, physical therapy, and medication management ordered by a physician. Home care is non-medical — companionship, meal prep, and personal hygiene. We provide both, but our specialty is clinical home healthcare.

How do you coordinate with my doctor?

Our clinical director communicates directly with your physician — sharing progress notes, lab results, vital trends, and care plan updates. We use secure electronic documentation and can integrate with most hospital and physician EHR systems for seamless continuity of care.

What conditions do you treat at home?

We provide home healthcare for a wide range of conditions: post-surgical recovery, cardiac rehabilitation, stroke recovery, diabetes management, COPD, wound care, IV antibiotic therapy, cancer care support, Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, and more. If you're unsure, our clinical team will assess eligibility at no cost.

Do your nurses speak Spanish or Creole?

Absolutely. South Florida is wonderfully diverse, and so is our clinical team. We have bilingual RNs and therapists fluent in Spanish, Haitian Creole, Portuguese, French, and more. We match language preferences whenever possible to ensure clear communication about medications, symptoms, and treatment plans.
